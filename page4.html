E-Waste Awareness

Understanding electronic waste & how to dispose it responsibly.

What is E-Waste?

E-waste (Electronic Waste) includes old, broken, or unused electronic devices like phones, laptops, chargers, TVs, and batteries. These items contain harmful chemicals like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can damage the environment if not disposed of properly.

Why Is E-Waste Harmful?

  • Releases toxic chemicals into soil and water.
  • Pollutes air when burned improperly.
  • Harms human health causing diseases & breathing problems.
  • Leads to soil contamination and crop damage.
  • Contains non-biodegradable materials like plastic and metal.

Examples of E-Waste

  • Mobile phones & chargers
  • Computers & laptops
  • Earphones, wires & cables
  • Batteries & power banks
  • Microwaves, TVs, refrigerators
  • Smartwatches & electronic toys

How Can We Reduce E-Waste?

  • Donate working electronics instead of throwing them.
  • Repair devices instead of buying new ones.
  • Use certified e-waste recycling centers.
  • Avoid burning electronic waste.

  • Choose long-lasting, durable electronics.
